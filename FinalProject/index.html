Ground Turkey Nachos

Ingredients

For the Nachos:
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 bag of tortilla chips (about 10-12 ounces)
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)

Prepare the Ground Turkey:

  2. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautÃ©ing until soft.
  3. Add the ground turkey, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until browned.
  4. Stir in cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. Cook for another 2-3 minutes. Set aside.

Make the Guacamole:
  1. Halve the avocados and remove the pits. Scoop the flesh into a bowl.
  2. Add the tomato, onion, jalapeÃ±o (if using), and lime juice.
  3. Mash everything together with a forkâ€”season with salt. Set aside.

Prepare the Pico de Gallo:
  1. In a separate bowl, combine tomatoes, onion, cilantro, jalapeÃ±o (if using), and lime juice.
  2. Season with salt. Set aside.

Assemble the Nachos:
  1. Preheat your oven to 375Â°F (190Â°C).
  2. Spread half of the tortilla chips in a large ovenproof dish or on a baking sheet.

Salmon & Cream Cheese Sushi

Ingredients

For Sushi Roll:
  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/3 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 200g fresh salmon (sashimi grade), thinly sliced
  • 100g cream cheese, cut into strips
For Topping:
  • Additional 100g fresh salmon (sashimi grade), thinly sliced
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
For Ponzu Sauce:
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
Equipment:
  • Bamboo sushi mat
  • Sharp knife
  • Rice cooker or pot

Prep and Cook Time


Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Overall Time Total Time (Prep + Cooking): Approximately 1 hour

Instructions


1. Cook the Sushi Rice:
Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
Cook the rice in a rice cooker or pot with 3 cups of water.
Once cooked, let it sit for about 10 minutes.
Gently fold in the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt.

2. Prepare the Ingredients:
Slice the salmon, cream cheese, cucumber, and avocado.
Chop the green onions.
Mix the ingredients for the ponzu sauce in a small bowl.

3. Assemble the Sushi Rolls:
Place a sheet of nori on the bamboo mat.
Spread a thin layer of rice over the nori, leaving about an inch at the top.
Lay salmon slices, cream cheese strips, cucumber, and avocado on the rice.
Using the bamboo mat, roll the sushi tightly from the bottom.

4. Top the Rolls:
Place additional salmon slices on top of each roll.
Use the bamboo mat to gently shape the roll and press the toppings in place.

5. Cut and Serve:
Using a sharp, wet knife - cut the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces.
Place the sushi on a plate and sprinkle with green onions.
Drizzle with ponzu sauce before serving.

Dairy-Free Creamy Garlic Salmon

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 cup coconut milk (full-fat for a creamier texture)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, to thicken the sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon water (if using cornstarch)
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional for color and a bit of smokiness)
  • Fresh herbs like dill or parsley, chopped
  • Lemon slices, for garnish

Prep and Cook Time

Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time:25 minutes
Total Time:30 minutes

Instructions

Prep:
  1. Season the salmon fillets with salt, pepper, and paprika (if using).
  2. If you choose to thicken the sauce with cornstarch, mix the cornstarch with 1 tablespoon of water in a small bowl to create a slurry. Set aside.

Cook:
  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the salmon fillets and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side or until golden and cooked to your preference. Remove the salmon from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add a bit more oil if needed, and sautÃ© the garlic and onions until fragrant and the onions are translucent about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Lower the heat to medium and pour in the coconut milk, stirring well. If youâ€™re using the cornstarch slurry, add it to the pan now, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens to your liking.
  4. Add the lemon juice and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.
  5. Return the salmon fillets to the skillet, pour the sauce over them, and cook for another 5 minutes to let the flavors meld.
  6. Garnish with fresh herbs and lemon slices before serving.

Serve the creamy garlic salmon with a side of steamed vegetables, over rice, or with a fresh salad for a complete meal.
Notes:
  • Ensure your coconut milk is unsweetened and doesn't contain any added flavors that might conflict with the dish.
  • You can also add a splash of white wine to the sauce before adding the coconut milk for an extra layer of flavor.
  • If you have more time and want to infuse the salmon with more flavor, you can marinate it in a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and herbs for about 15-30 minutes before cooking.

Simple Tonkatsu Ramen

Ingredients

  • Tonkatsu Ramen Broth: 2 - We recommend the Good & Gather Ramen Broth
  • Pork Belly Slices or Pork Loin: 4 thin slices
  • Soy Sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Garlic: 2 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1 teaspoon, grated
  • Vegetable Oil: for frying
  • Eggs: 2
  • Green Onions: 2, finely chopped
  • Nori Seaweed Sheets: 2, cut into halves
  • Bamboo Shoots or Corn: for topping
  • Sesame Seeds: for garnish
  • Butter: 1 tablespoon (optional)
  • Chili Oil or Sesame Oil: for flavor (optional)

Prep and Cook Time

Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 35 minutes

Instructions

  • Quality of Broth: Choose a good quality at-home tonkatsu ramen broth base.
  • Variations: Feel free to add other toppings like bean sprouts, bok choy, or mushrooms.
  • Vegetarian Option: Skip the pork and use tofu or extra vegetables. Look for a vegetarian ramen packet.
  • Storing: It's best enjoyed fresh. However, you can store the broth and toppings separately in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
